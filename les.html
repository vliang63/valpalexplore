Neighborhood Lowdown

LES is a neighborhood that has been popularized only in recent years. The divde between LES and Chinatown is blurry, and you'll see here the ghettoness of Chinatown mixed in with trendy places that cater towards younger folks who have money to spend. There are tons of bars at a more reasonable price than Meatpacking and often with more character as well.

Public Transit Service: A, C, E

Cup of Joe

12 Corners - 155 East Broadway

I found Penelopes serving Kobrick's coffee, which they also serve here at 12 Corners. If you're looking for a wide selection of good coffee, this is a great place to go.

Let's get started

Eat

Hotel Chantelle
#brunch #rooftop #casual #bottomless

Hotel Chantelle is known for its nighttime activities and dance floor, which can get a little grungy at times, but in the day they serve a nice rooftop brunch with live jazz at an affordable price. Bottomless drinks are something around $6 (which scares me), but it's a great environment for a group brunch!

Drink

Back Room
#speakeasy #historic

Of all the speakeasys in NYC, this one is my favorite because it is a real speakeasy from the age of Prohibition. The picture that I've posted is the actual entrance, which is made more conspicuous because there is usually a random guy standing by the gate (to check IDs). Cocktails are served in tea cups (because it's an establishment that doesn't serve alcohol, remember?!) and occasionally they'll have live Big Band music and swing dancers.

Apotheke
#speakeasy

While Back Room has the authenticity, Apotheke probably has the best looking front because it's hidden deep in Chinatown. What you'll see about the inside is really nothing you'd expect from the outside, and all the cocktails are pharmacy themed. They have a pineapple one that's very tasty!

Loreley
#outdoor #biergarten

If you're ever missing home, you can try this place perhaps (even though there is a pretty real looking version of Hofbrauhouse in Midtown East that you probably wouldn't want to go to). In any case, it's a nice place to sit outside for a drink and is in a neighborhood that is a little more quiet.

Recommended Trip

Grab brunch at Hotel Chantelle.

Take a walk through SoHo and grab dinner in Chinatown.

End up in LES at the Backroom for a casual drink around 8pm when it opens.
